Akash Chaudhary

UX Researcher
Authentic User Experience Lab, University of California, Santa Cruz

I am interested in exploring the values and motivations behind the habit formation of people around the use of existing and novel technologies. My projects center around exploring the design space for enabling and affording better emotional expressions in the day to day lives of humans.

I am currently exploring the experiences of people living in rural areas. We realized that many people like living in rural areas of California as they relish the sense of quiet and calm solitude, natural beauty, extended personal space, and care and warmth offerend in the close-knit communities. These people usually try to find cheap rural areas not too far from the San Francisco bay area where they can travel and work when required. This sort of personal and working symbiosis exposes people to certain gaps in infrastructure facilities, like computing and transportation in and around the rural areas. We intend to explore some of these shortcomings in the rural infrastructure by designing an interactive artifact that engages with people in meaningful ways.

In my previous projects, I analyzed user habits on video streaming platforms, specifically exploring compulsive online behaviors. The project revealed the role UI design elements that act as user impediments to digital wellbeing on online platforms. I also realized that English speaking habits in non-native speakers is flawed and problematic. Subsequently I designed and tested the performance of a speech improvement mobile application through iterative design phases. I am currently seeking job opportunities as a UX researcher.
